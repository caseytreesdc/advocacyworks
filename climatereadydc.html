Advocacy Works: Climate Ready DC Plan

The Problem: Heat & Flooding

Climate change experts are predicting hotter summers and heavier rains in Washington, D.C.

The Solution: Trees

Research shows that trees increase climate change resilience in cities.

Tree canopies contribute to reduced temperatures while their root systems absorb volumes of stormwater. Impervious surfaces like pavements, concrete and rooftops seal the soil surface and creates polluted runoff of 55 percent or more.

In contrast natural ground cover absorbs stormwater and filters it through trees' established root systems resulting in less than 10 percent runoff. Leaves also capture rainfall like tiny cups, and through evapotranspiration, cool surrounding air.

Action: Climate Ready D.C.

With input from our advocates, D.C. officials drafted a plan to establish climate adaptation strategies

Drafted in 2016, Climate Ready DC is the District's plan to prepare for heavy rainfall and flooding, extreme temperatures, and rising sea levels in the coming decades. Our tree advocates collaborated with D.C.'s Department of Energy & Environment to include more tree-specific solutions to keep our city cool and dry:

ACTION 1

Plant trees in raingardens to absorb even more stormwater

ACTION 2

Plant shade trees to keep homes and buildings cool

ACTION 3

Keep street tree boxes filled to shade pavement

ACTION 4

Plant tree along riverbanks to keep soil stable during floods
